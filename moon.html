The Moon Is a Great Timekeeper

Ancient civilizations used the Moon’s phases to track time and create calendars, giving rise to lunar months that are still observed in various cultures today.

Birth Of Moon

The Moon is believed to have formed about 4.5 billion years ago through a process known as the giant impact hypothesis. This theory suggests that shortly after the Earth was formed, it collided with a Mars-sized body named Theia. This colossal impact was so powerful that it ejected a significant amount of material from Earth’s outer layers into orbit around our planet. This debris began to coalesce due to gravitational attraction, forming a disk around the Earth, which eventually aggregated to become the Moon.
