MERCURY VENUS EARTH MARS JUPITER SATURN URANUS NEPTUNE

Distance from Sun : Mercury is the first Planet which is located at a distance of 57.9 Million Kilometers from Sun.

Orbit Size : Mercury Orbital eccentricity is 0.206 that means it has highly elliptical compared to other planets.

Natural Satellites : Mercury has no Moons or no Natural Satellites.

Artificial Satellites :

  • Maginer(1974-1975)
  • Bepi Colombo-Launched in 2018 -- It is a joint mission of ESA and it will study Mercury in greater detail and is set to arrive by 2025.

Mercury's year (revolution time around the Sun): 88 Earth days. It is the fastest orbiting planet in the solar system.

Resources on Mercury : While no resources have been exploited from Mercury yet, some significant features of its composition include: Metal-rich planet, Water Ice, Exosphere Composition.

Distance from Sun : Venus is located at a distance of 108.2 million kilometers (67.2 million miles) from Sun.

Orbit Size : Orbital Eccentricity - 0.007, meaning Venus has one of the most circular orbits in the solar system.

Natural Satellites : Venus has no moons or natural satellites.

Artificial Satellites : Several spacecraft have visited or orbited Venus -

  • Venera Program (USSR)
  • Pioneer Venus Orbiter (NASA, 1978–1992)
  • Magellan (NASA, 1989–1994)

Resources on Venus : Venus is a rocky planet with some interesting features, but due to its extreme surface conditions (temperatures around 465°C (869°F) and atmospheric pressure 92 times that of Earth), extracting resources would be very difficult.

Fun facts of Venus : Hottest Planet in the Solar System Despite not being the closest planet to the Sun, Venus is the hottest planet due to its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide, which creates a runaway greenhouse effect. Surface temperatures reach a scorching 465°C (869°F)—hot enough to melt lead!

Distance from Sun : Earth is located at a distance of 149.6 million kilometers (93 million miles) from Sun. This distance is also called 1 Astronomical Unit (AU), which is the baseline used to measure distances within our solar system.

Orbit Size : Orbital Eccentricity - Earth’s orbit is nearly circular with a small eccentricity of 0.0167, meaning the variation between perihelion and aphelion is relatively small.

Natural Satellites : Natural Satellites: Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon, which is the fifth largest moon in the solar system.

Artificial Satellites : Earth is surrounded by thousands of artificial satellites, with many more in orbit for communication, weather forecasting, GPS, scientific research, and space exploration. Notable examples include -

  • International Space Station (ISS)
  • Hubble Space Telescope
  • GPS Satellites

Resources on Earth : Earth is rich in a vast variety of natural resources, making it uniquely suitable for life. Some key resources include -

  • Water, Atmosphere, Mineral Resources, Metals, Metals, Renewable Energy Resources.

Fun facts of Earth : We’re Spinning Faster Than You Think! The Earth spins on its axis at about 1,670 kilometers per hour (1,040 miles per hour) at the equator! However, you don’t feel this motion because the atmosphere moves with us at the same speed.

Distance from Sun : Mars is located at a distance of 227.9 million kilometers (141.6 million miles) from Sun.

Orbit Size : Orbital eccentricity - Mars has a more eccentric (elliptical) orbit compared to Earth, with an eccentricity of 0.0934.

Artificial Satellites : Mars is currently orbited by several spacecraft, which study its atmosphere, surface, and climate.

Natural Satellites :Mars has two moons -

  • Phobos: The larger and innermost moon, orbits very close to Mars, completing a full orbit in just 7.66 hours.
  • Deimos: The smaller and more distant moon, takes about 30.35 hours to complete an orbit around Mars.

Resources on Mars : Mars has some potentially valuable resources that could support future human missions and colonization efforts. These include -

  • Water Ice, Carbon Dioxide, Regolith, Methane, In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU)

Fun facts of Mars : Mars is Half the Size of Earth Mars has a diameter of about 6,779 kilometers (4,212 miles), making it roughly half the size of Earth. Its surface area is similar to the total surface area of Earth’s dry land.

Distance from Sun : Jupiter is located at a distance of 778.5 million kilometers (484 million miles) from Sun.

Orbit Size : Orbital eccentricity - Jupiter has a slightly elliptical orbit with an eccentricity of 0.0489, making it one of the less eccentric orbits among the planets.

Natural Satellites : Jupiter has 79 known moons, making it the planet with the most moons in the solar system. The four largest moons are known as the Galilean moons, discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610: -

  • Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto

Artificial Satellites : Numerous spacecraft have studied Jupiter, including -

  • Pioneer 10 and 11, Voyager 1 and 2, Galileo Orbiter, Juno.

Resources on Jupiter : Jupiter is a gas giant and does not have a solid surface, which limits the types of resources we typically associate with terrestrial planets like Earth or Mercury. However, it does have some intriguing characteristics that suggest potential resources -

  • Hydrogen and Helium, Ammonia and Methane, Water, Hydrocarbon Compounds, Gravitational Resources.

Fun facts of Jupiter : The Giant of the Solar System Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, with a diameter of about 139,820 kilometers (86,881 miles)—more than 11 times that of Earth! It is so massive that it accounts for about 70% of the total planetary mass in our solar system.

Distance from Sun : Saturn is located at a distance of 1.43 billion kilometers (890 million miles) from Sun.

Orbit Size : Orbital eccentricity - Saturn's orbit is slightly elliptical, with an eccentricity of 0.0565.

Natural Satellites : Saturn has 83 known moons, with several noteworthy ones: Titan: The largest moon of Saturn and the second-largest moon in the solar system. It has a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane. Rhea: The second-largest moon, which has a heavily cratered surface. Iapetus: Known for its two-tone coloration; one side is very dark, and the other is bright. Dione: Features bright wispy markings and a mix of craters and smooth areas.

Artificial Satellites : Numerous spacecraft have studied Saturn, including -

  • Pioneer 11, Voyager 1 and 2, Cassini-Huygens

Resources on Saturn : Saturn, being a gas giant, does not have a solid surface, which limits the resources typically associated with terrestrial planets like Earth or Mercury. However, it does have some intriguing characteristics that suggest potential resources.

Fun facts of Saturn : Saturn is best known for its stunning rings, which are made mostly of ice particles, along with smaller amounts of rocky debris and dust. These rings extend outward from the planet up to 282,000 kilometers (175,000 miles) but are only about 10 meters (30 feet) thick!

Orbit Size : Orbital eccentricity - Uranus has a low orbital eccentricity of about 0.046, indicating a nearly circular orbit.

Artificial Satellites : The only spacecraft to have visited Uranus is Voyager 2, which conducted a flyby in 1986, providing much of our current knowledge about the planet and its moons.

Resources on Uranus : Water, Ammonia, and Methane: Uranus's atmosphere contains significant amounts of water, ammonia, and methane. Methane gives the planet its blue color and could be of interest for chemical processes.

Fun facts of Uranus : The Tilted Giant Uranus is known for its extreme axial tilt of 98 degrees. This means it essentially rotates on its side, making it the only planet in the solar system that rotates almost perpendicular to its orbit.

Distance from Sun : Neptune is located at a distance of 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles) from Sun.

Orbit Size : Orbital eccentricity - Neptune has a low orbital eccentricity of about 0.009, indicating a nearly circular orbit.

Natural Satellites : Neptune has 14 known moons, with some of the most notable ones being -

  • Triton, Proteus, Nereid, Larissa

Artificial Satellites : The only spacecraft to have visited Neptune is Voyager 2, which conducted a flyby in 1989, providing much of the current knowledge about the planet and its moons.

Resources on Neptune : Neptune, like Uranus, is classified as an ice giant. While it does not have resources in the same sense as terrestrial planets like Earth or Mercury, here are some notable aspects - Water, Ammonia, and Methane

Fun facts of Neptune : Discovered by Calculation Neptune was discovered on September 23, 1846, not by direct observation but through mathematical predictions. Astronomers used calculations based on irregularities in the orbit of Uranus to locate Neptune.
